About the Course

A BS (Bachelor of Science) in Social Work is an undergraduate degree preparing individuals for direct practice helping people, families, groups, and communities address social issues, develop skills, and access resources, combining classroom learning on human behavior, welfare policy, and ethics with mandatory real-world field experience (internships) to promote social justice and well-being. It's a pathway for entry-level roles in social services, focusing on enhancing people's functioning and linking them to support networks.
